Section A: Concept Cards

Heet Lakhani (124BTCM1105)

1. Tokenization

Definition: Breaking down text into smaller units (tokens) like words or subwords.

Purpose: Fundamental step to make text processable by algorithms.

Example: "Hello world" → ["Hello", "world"]

Heet Lakhani (124BTCM1105)

2. Bag of Words (BoW)

Definition: A simple representation model that counts word frequencies regardless of order.

Why: Provides a numerical basis for classification tasks.

Limitations: Ignores grammar, structure, and context.

Heet Lakhani (124BTCM1105)

3. Word Embeddings

Definition: Dense vector representation of words in continuous vector space.

How: Maps similar words to nearby points in geometric space.

Application: Sentiment analysis, recommendation engines.

Anand Raghvani (124BTCM1113)

4. Transformer-based Representations

Definition: Models using self-attention to capture long-range dependencies.

Working: Learns context by weighing the influence of all words in a sequence simultaneously.

Application: Modern LLMs (BERT, GPT).

Anand Raghvani (124BTCM1113)

5. Case Normalization

Definition: Converting all text to a uniform case (usually lowercase).

Purpose: Reduces vocabulary size; treats "Apple" and "apple" as the same token.

Example: "NLP is Great" → "nlp is great"
